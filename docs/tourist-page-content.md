## Inside the Firehouse

### Section Intro
Most visitors only see the outside. Here's what's behind the big red doors.

### Gallery Items (4-6 photos needed)

**The Pole**
Yes, it's real. Yes, it still works. It runs from the third floor all the way down to the garage — just like in the movie.

**The Spiral Staircase**
The original cast iron staircase the firefighters used. Mia walked these same steps.

**The Princess Tower**
The hose-drying tower is now an art studio. On a clear day you can see downtown, the bay, and the hills of the Excelsior.

**The Garage**
Where Engine No. 43 once parked. Now it hosts art markets, music, and community gatherings.

## The Door: Resist Much, Obey Little

### Headline
Princesses can be rebels.

### Body
The two women on our door are Princess Mia — pre-transformation, wild curly hair intact — and her sister in spirit, Princess Leia.

We made this during the first Trump administration, when neighbors were marching together and looking out for each other. The message still holds.

This is the Excelsior — a neighborhood of immigrants, families, and working people. We watch out for each other here. We warn our neighbors. We show up.

Take a photo. Share it. The more people see it, the further the message travels.

## Design Notes for Claude Code

- Use warm, textured backgrounds (paper grain overlay)
- Gallery should be a simple grid or masonry layout
- The door section should feature the "Resist Much Obey Little" photo prominently
- Holy Moly section: use the drone photo full-width or near full-width
- Newsletter form: simple embed (Buttondown/Mailchimp) or native form
- Keep the listening room link subtle — small text at the very bottom: "Interested in our listening room sessions? Learn more →"
- Mobile-first: most tourists will be scanning the QR on their phones
